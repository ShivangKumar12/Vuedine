🏪 POS = Point of Sale

A Restaurant POS (Point of Sale) system is the central nervous system of a restaurant. It connects every operation — customer ordering → kitchen preparation → billing → payment — into one unified, automated flow. Unlike a simple billing machine, modern POS systems are cloud-based software platforms that manage the entire restaurant lifecycle from a single screen.

🔄 How a Single Order Triggers the Whole System

When a customer places an order (via QR code, waiter app, or counter), ONE transaction simultaneously: deducts ingredients from inventory, prints/displays KOT in kitchen, updates the table status, adds to sales reports, records customer data in CRM, and syncs to accounting. This is why a POS is not just software — it's infrastructure.

💡 Why Restaurants Need Your System

India has 7.5 million+ food businesses. Most still use manual billing or outdated systems. The rise of QR-based contactless ordering post-COVID created massive demand. Your target: restaurants that want to eliminate dependency on waiters for order-taking, reduce errors, speed up service, and get real-time analytics — all in one affordable platform.

QR-Based Ordering System

Your #1 feature — the complete end-to-end flow explained

✅ Your Core Innovation: No App Download Required

Customer scans QR code with their phone camera → Browser opens instantly → Full menu loads → Customer orders → Order lands on restaurant POS panel. Zero friction. Works on any smartphone. No Android/iOS app install needed. This is the exact model customers want in 2025.

1

QR Code Generation (Per Table)

Your system generates a unique QR code for every table in the restaurant. Each QR encodes a URL like: https://menu.yourapp.com/r/{restaurantId}/t/{tableId}. This URL contains both the restaurant identity and the table number. QR codes are printable on standees, table mats, tent cards, or stickers. The restaurant owner can regenerate QR codes anytime from the admin panel.

2

Customer Scans — Menu Opens in Browser

Customer points their phone camera at the QR code. The phone recognizes it and opens a Progressive Web App (PWA) in the browser — no app installation required. The menu loads with restaurant branding, food photos, categories, prices, and item descriptions. The page is fully mobile-optimized and supports Veg/Non-Veg filters, dietary tags, bestseller highlights, and search functionality.

3

Customer Browses & Adds to Cart

Customer scrolls through menu categories, taps items, sees descriptions & photos, selects quantities, chooses add-ons/variants (spice level, size, toppings), and adds special instructions. A sticky cart button shows running total. Customer can modify the cart freely before placing order. No login required — completely anonymous session.

4

Order Placed — Instant Confirmation

Customer taps "Place Order". The app sends the order via WebSocket or REST API to your backend. The customer sees an animated "Order Placed! ✅" screen with order number and estimated time. They can continue adding more items. Multiple rounds of ordering from the same table are fully supported — each round creates a new KOT that gets added to the running bill.

5

Order Appears on Restaurant Panel (Live)

Simultaneously, the order appears on the Restaurant Admin Panel (desktop/tablet) with table number, items ordered, quantities, and special instructions. A notification sound/alert triggers. The waiter sees the table is now "active". The POS staff can accept the order (or it auto-accepts based on settings).

6

KOT Fires to Kitchen

The system automatically generates a Kitchen Order Ticket (KOT) with table number, order time, items, and special notes. This KOT either: (a) prints on a kitchen printer, or (b) displays on the Kitchen Display System (KDS) screen, or (c) both. Items can be routed to different stations — hot kitchen, cold kitchen, bar — based on category.

7

Food Served — Bill Generated

Kitchen marks items as "Ready". Waiter delivers food. Customer can request bill via the QR menu ("Request Bill" button) or waiter prints it. The POS generates a GST-compliant bill with itemized charges, applicable taxes (CGST/SGST/IGST), discounts, service charge, and total. Supports digital bill via WhatsApp/SMS.

8

Payment — Multiple Modes

Customer pays via Cash, Card (Razorpay/PayU/Stripe), UPI (GPay/PhonePe/Paytm), or online payment via the QR menu itself (scan QR on bill → pay directly). Bill is settled, table is freed automatically. Payment record syncs to reports, inventory deductions are confirmed, and CRM is updated with customer visit data.

Kitchen Display System (KDS)

Digital replacement for paper KOTs — the smart kitchen brain

🟠 Important
📺

What is KDS and Why You Need It

KDS replaces paper KOT slips with a digital screen in the kitchen. Orders appear instantly on a monitor, chefs tap items as they're prepared, and the system tracks timing. It eliminates paper waste, reduces confusion, speeds up kitchen operations, and allows management to track kitchen efficiency in real-time.

  • Orders display with table number & time
  • Color-coding: new (green) → preparing (yellow) → done (grey)
  • Sound alert for new orders
  • Tap to mark item as "done"
  • Timer shows how long each order has been waiting
  • Priority flags for rush orders
  • Station-wise display (hot, cold, bar)
  • Multiple KDS screens supported
  • Works on any Android tablet/TV
  • Bump bar support for chef ergonomics
  • Kitchen performance reports (avg time)
  • Offline mode — works without internet
⚠️ KDS vs Printer — Give Restaurants the Choice

Some restaurants prefer physical KOT printers (traditional, familiar, no electricity concern). Others prefer KDS screens (eco-friendly, real-time updates). Your system must support BOTH simultaneously. Large restaurants may have a printer AND a KDS screen in the same kitchen. Never force one option.

Inventory & Stock Management

Know exactly what's in your kitchen at all times — prevent wastage and stockouts

🟠 Important
📦

Smart Inventory Engine

Every time an order is placed, the system automatically deducts the raw materials used from stock — based on the recipe defined for each dish. No manual stock tracking needed. The system tells you when you're running low, helps you order from suppliers, and tracks wastage.

🥦 Raw Material Tracking
  • Add raw materials (chicken, oil, rice, spices)
  • Define unit of measurement (kg, litre, piece)
  • Set minimum stock alert threshold
  • Auto-deduct when dish is ordered
  • Recipe management (each dish = list of ingredients)
  • Multi-stage recipes (base sauce → dish)
  • Food wastage tracking
  • Supplier management
  • Purchase order generation
  • Stock-in entries from purchases
📊 Inventory Reports
  • Current stock levels dashboard
  • Low stock alerts (push notification)
  • Daily consumption report
  • Wastage report by period
  • Purchase vs consumption analysis
  • Food cost % per dish
  • Gross margin per item
  • Slow-moving items report
  • Stock valuation report
  • Supplier-wise purchase history
💡 Recipe Management = Cost Control

Define: "1 plate Butter Chicken = 200g chicken + 50g butter + 30g cream + spices". Every time this dish is sold, the system deducts these exact quantities from stock. This gives the owner real-time food cost data, catches pilferage (theoretical vs actual consumption gap), and prevents running out of key ingredients during service.

🟠 Important

Waiter App — Tablet/Phone-Based Ordering

Even with QR ordering, restaurants need a waiter/captain app for situations where customers are not comfortable with self-ordering, or the restaurant type requires attended service. The waiter carries an Android phone/tablet and takes orders directly at the table — replacing the traditional notepad. Orders go straight to kitchen without visiting the billing counter.

  • Full menu access on mobile
  • Table selection from floor map
  • Take order for specific table
  • Add items to running order
  • Send to kitchen (KOT) instantly
  • Mark food as delivered
  • Request bill with one tap
  • See all active tables
  • Receive kitchen "ready" notifications
  • Works offline (syncs when online)
  • Login with waiter credentials
  • Waiter-wise order tracking for tips
💡 QR Ordering + Waiter App = Perfect Combination

The ideal restaurant workflow: 70% of customers self-order via QR → 30% order via waiter app (for elderly, families, etc.). Your system handles both simultaneously. The kitchen sees all orders in one unified KDS/printer — regardless of whether they came from QR or the waiter app.

This is a major value proposition: "Use our QR system for direct orders and save lakhs in commission annually."

Backend / Server

🟢 API Server
Node.js + Express.js (or NestJS)
Fast, scalable REST API. NestJS adds structure and TypeScript for large teams.
⚡ Real-time
Socket.io (WebSocket)
Push orders to KDS, admin panel, and waiter app in real-time without polling. Critical for live kitchen updates.
🗄️ Database
PostgreSQL (primary) + Redis (cache)
PostgreSQL for all business data (orders, inventory, billing). Redis for session management, real-time state, queue.
📁 File Storage
AWS S3 / Cloudflare R2
Store food photos, receipts, reports. Cloudflare R2 has zero egress cost — better for Indian startups.
🔐 Auth
JWT + Role-Based Access Control
Secure login for each role (Owner, Manager, Waiter, Chef). JWT tokens with refresh logic.

Infrastructure & Integrations

☁️ Hosting
AWS / Google Cloud + Vercel (frontend)

💳 Payments
Razorpay (primary) + PayU
India's best payment gateway. Supports UPI, cards, wallets, payment links. Easy API integration.
💬 WhatsApp
Twilio / Meta WhatsApp Business API
Send bills, loyalty updates, OTPs via WhatsApp. Critical for Indian restaurant communication.
📱 SMS
MSG91 / Textlocal (India)
Bulk SMS for marketing, OTP verification. India-specific providers with DLT compliance.
📊 Analytics
Custom Analytics + Metabase
Build custom analytics engine. Use Metabase or Superset for business intelligence dashboards.
📦 Queue
Bull.js (Redis Queue) / RabbitMQ
Background jobs: print jobs, SMS queues, report generation, inventory updates without blocking API.

⚠️ Critical Success Factors

1. Offline Mode is Non-Negotiable — Indian restaurants have unreliable internet. Your system MUST work offline and sync when connectivity returns.
2. Hardware Compatibility — Support standard thermal printers (ESC/POS protocol). Restaurants already own these; they won't buy new hardware.
3. Onboarding — Petpooja needs on-site training. Beat them with guided self-onboarding, video tutorials, and live WhatsApp support.
4. Speed — The POS counter handles 100+ orders per hour during peak time. Your system must be fast. Lag = lost customers + unhappy staff.
5. Data Security — Customer data, payment data, and business data must be encrypted and backed up. Restaurants cannot afford data loss.
